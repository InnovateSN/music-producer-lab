16th-Note Groove:
More Movement

üéµ What Are Sixteenth Notes?

Sixteenth notes (1/16) divide each beat into four parts. If we count quarters as "1, 2, 3, 4", sixteenths are: 1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a 4 e & a.

  • Quarter notes: 4 per bar
  • Eighth notes: 8 per bar
  • Sixteenth notes: 16 per bar (the fastest common subdivision)
Grid mapping: In a 16-step sequencer, each step IS a sixteenth note!

1Ô∏è‚É£ Step 1: Understand the Grid

Your 16-step grid represents one bar of sixteenth notes. Each beat spans 4 steps:

  • Beat 1: Steps 1-4 (1, e, &, a)
  • Beat 2: Steps 5-8 (2, e, &, a)
  • Beat 3: Steps 9-12 (3, e, &, a)
  • Beat 4: Steps 13-16 (4, e, &, a)

2Ô∏è‚É£ Step 2: Add Strategic 16th Notes

Instead of playing all 16 hi-hats (which can sound busy), we'll add one extra sixteenth note to create anticipation before beat 3.

  • Keep your eighth-note hi-hats: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15
  • Add step 8 (the "a" of beat 2)
  • This creates a push into the snare on beat 3

The pattern "rolls" into the third beat, creating a more modern, danceable feel.
