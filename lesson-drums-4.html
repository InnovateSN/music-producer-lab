What Are Sixteenth Notes?

Divide each beat into four parts.

Sixteenth notes (1/16) divide each beat into four parts. If we count quarters as "1, 2, 3, 4", sixteenths are: 1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a 4 e & a.

  • Quarter notes: 4 per bar (the main beats).
  • Eighth notes: 8 per bar (twice as fast).
  • Sixteenth notes: 16 per bar (the fastest common subdivision).

Grid mapping: In a 16-step sequencer, each step IS a sixteenth note!

Step 1

Understand the Grid.

Your 16-step grid represents one bar of sixteenth notes. Each beat spans 4 steps:

  • Beat 1: Steps 1-4 (1, e, &, a)
  • Beat 2: Steps 5-8 (2, e, &, a)
  • Beat 3: Steps 9-12 (3, e, &, a)
  • Beat 4: Steps 13-16 (4, e, &, a)
Step 2

Add Strategic 16th Notes.

Instead of playing all 16 hi-hats (which can sound busy), we'll add one extra sixteenth note to create anticipation before beat 3.

  • Keep your eighth-note hi-hats: steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15.
  • Add step 8 (the "a" of beat 2).
  • This creates a push into the snare on beat 3.

  • Listen: The pattern "rolls" into the third beat, creating a more modern, danceable feel.

Exercise

Create the 16th-Note Groove

Add a sixteenth-note hi-hat to create anticipation and movement in your beat.

What to do:

  1. Kick row: Click steps 1, 5, 9, 13 to place your kicks.
  2. Snare row: Click steps 5 and 13 to place your snares.
  3. Hi-Hat row: Click steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 8, 9, 11, 13, 15 (eighth notes + step 8).
  4. Press Play and notice how step 8 adds energy!
  5. When satisfied, click Check Exercise to verify your pattern.
