What is the Backbeat?

The rhythmic backbone of modern music.

The backbeat is one of the most important concepts in modern drumming. It's the snare drum hitting on beats 2 and 4 of every bar.

  • Beat 1: The "downbeat" ‚Äì where the bar starts (kick)
  • Beat 2: First backbeat (snare) ‚Äì creates tension
  • Beat 3: Secondary downbeat (kick)
  • Beat 4: Second backbeat (snare) ‚Äì resolves and pushes forward

This push-and-pull between kick and snare is what makes music feel groovy and danceable.

Step 1

Start with the Kick Pattern.

Begin with the "4 on the floor" kick pattern from Lesson 1. This establishes the steady pulse that the snare will play against.

  • Set the tempo to 120 BPM.
  • Place kicks on steps 1, 5, 9, 13 (beats 1, 2, 3, 4).
  • Make sure the loop is playing continuously.
Step 2

Add the Snare on the Backbeats.

Now add snare hits on the backbeats. In a 16-step grid, beat 2 starts at step 5, and beat 4 starts at step 13.

  • Add a snare on step 5 (beat 2).
  • Add a snare on step 13 (beat 4).
  • Notice how kick and snare hit together on beats 2 and 4.

  • Listen: The snare creates a powerful "BAP" on 2 and 4 that drives the groove.

The snare landing with the kick on beats 2 and 4 creates a powerful, punchy feel. This is the foundation of almost every genre!
