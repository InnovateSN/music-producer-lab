You have 7 seconds to grab attention. Your intro must establish the vibe, hint at what's coming, and make listeners want more.

  • Start with the hook: Tease your main melody or vocal immediately
  • Signature sound: Use a unique sound that defines your track
  • Build anticipation: Strip elements down, then gradually add
  • Keep it short: 4-16 bars maximum for most genres
  • Filter opening: Classic techniqueâ€”low-pass filter that opens up

  • Callback to intro: Bring back intro elements for bookend effect
  • Fade out: Classic technique, works for many genres
  • Hard stop: Abrupt ending for dramatic effect
  • DJ-friendly loop: End with drums only for mixing
  • Resolve tension: End on the root note/chord for closure
