Mastering Compression: Glue Without Crushing

Mastering compression serves a different purpose than mix compression. Instead of shaping individual sounds, you're gently "gluing" the entire mix together, controlling overall dynamics, and adding subtle density. The key word is gentle—typically 1-3 dB of gain reduction with slow attack and release times. Multiband compression lets you control different frequency ranges independently, solving problems that broadband compression can't address.

Glue Compression

Mastering compressors "glue" the mix, making elements feel cohesive. Use slow attack (30-100 ms) to preserve transients and auto/slow release. Ratio 1.5:1 to 3:1. Aim for 1-3 dB gain reduction on peaks. If you're compressing more, you're crushing.

Multiband Compression

Multiband splits the signal into frequency bands, compressing each independently. Use it to tighten loose bass without affecting highs, or control harsh vocals without dulling the mix. Crossover points are critical—poor placement causes phase issues.

Parallel Compression

Blend heavily compressed signal with the original for density without losing dynamics. Set up a send to a crushed compressor (10:1+, fast attack/release) and blend it low. Adds body and excitement while keeping transients from the dry signal.

Typical Settings

Ratio: 1.5:1 to 3:1. Attack: 30-100 ms. Release: Auto or 100-300 ms. Threshold: Set for 1-3 dB GR on peaks. Knee: Soft for transparency. If it sounds compressed, you've gone too far.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Over-Compression: More than 3-4 dB gain reduction in mastering usually means you're crushing dynamics. The limiter should handle loudness—let the compressor just glue.
Fast Attack Killing Transients: Fast attack times (under 10 ms) clamp down on transients, making drums sound flat and lifeless. Mastering compression should let transients through.
Multiband Without Purpose: Don't use multiband compression just because you have it. Each band introduces phase relationships. Only use it when you have a specific problem to solve.

Why This Matters

  • Cohesion: Glue compression makes the mix feel like one unified piece, not separate elements.
  • Consistent Energy: Gentle dynamics control keeps energy even throughout the song.
  • Headroom Management: Compression tames peaks, giving your limiter an easier job for cleaner loudness.
  • Problem Solving: Multiband lets you fix frequency-specific dynamic issues the mixer missed.
