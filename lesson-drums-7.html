Concept

What Are Ghost Notes?

Ghost notes are very soft drum hits played between the main beats. They add "shuffle" and "pocket" without changing the core pattern.

  • Ghost (15-40 velocity): Barely audible texture on off-beats
  • Accent (100-127 velocity): Emphasized hits on main beats
  • Dynamic range: The contrast between soft and loud creates life
Technique

Where to Place Ghost Notes

Ghost notes typically go on the off-beats (the "e" and "a" of 16th notes).

  • Before backbeat: Ghost on the "a" of 1 and 3
  • After backbeat: Ghost on the "e" of 2 and 4
  • Continuous: Ghost on every 16th except main hits
Exercise

Add Ghost Notes to Your Groove

Transform a basic backbeat into a professional groove by using velocity control.

What to do:

  1. Kick: Steps 1, 5, 9, 13 (four-on-the-floor foundation)
  2. Snare: Steps 5 and 13 (backbeat at high velocity)
  3. Hi-Hat: Steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15 (8th notes)
Tip: Ghost notes should be felt, not heard prominently. They add groove without competing with main hits.
Target Pattern:
Kick: 1234 5678 9101112 13141516
Snare: 1234 5678 9101112 13141516
Hi-Hat: 1234 5678 9101112 13141516
Focus on velocity: accents on main beats, soft ghost notes on off-beats
