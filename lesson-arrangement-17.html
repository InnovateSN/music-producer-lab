Sync & Media: Arranging for Film, TV & Advertising

Sync licensing places your music in films, TV shows, commercials, and video games. It's a lucrative market, but it requires specific arrangement considerations. Music supervisors need flexibility—tracks that can be edited, shortened, or remixed to fit visual content. Creating sync-ready versions of your music opens doors to significant placement opportunities.

Multiple Versions

Create 60s, 30s, and 15s edits. Music supervisors often need specific lengths. Also provide full-length, instrumental-only, and vocal-only versions. More versions = more placement opportunities.

Clean Stems

Export separate stems—drums, bass, synths, vocals, FX. This allows editors to mix under dialogue or adjust levels for different scenes. Standard practice for professional sync placements.

Strong Hooks Fast

Get to the hook in 10-15 seconds. Commercials are short. Trailers need instant impact. Long intros don't work for sync. Create alternate versions with immediate impact.

Clearable Content

100% original content only. No samples that aren't cleared, no interpolations, no covers. Sync deals require clean copyright. One uncleared sample kills the entire placement.

Sync-Ready Checklist

  • Full version: Complete song with natural ending
  • Instrumental: No vocals for dialogue flexibility
  • Short edits: 60s, 30s, 15s cuts that feel complete
  • Stems: Individual track groups for re-mixing
  • Metadata: Tempo, key, mood, genre tags included
