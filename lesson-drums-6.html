Step 1

What is syncopation?

Syncopation shifts emphasis from strong beats to unexpected positions between beats, creating movement and groove.

  • The kick doesn’t always land on the quarters: it can hit on the “&” or selected sixteenth notes.
  • Leaving space between hits increases rhythmic tension.
  • Listen and feel where the pattern “makes you move.”
Step 2

Kick pattern for this lesson.

The target for this lesson is a syncopated kick pattern (example): chosen steps (1, 3, 6, 11) in the 16-step grid.

  • In a 16-step grid: suggested target steps: 1, 3, 6, 11.
  • Snare: keep the backbeat on 5 and 13.
  • Hi-hat: eighths to maintain the pulse.
