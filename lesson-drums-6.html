What Is Syncopation?

Accents on unexpected beats.

Syncopation means placing accents on unexpected beats ‚Äì between or before where you naturally expect them. This creates tension and groove.

  • Expected: Kicks on beats 1, 2, 3, 4 (4-on-the-floor)
  • Syncopated: Kicks on off-beats or "&"s
  • Effect: Creates forward motion and "groove"
  • Used in: Funk, hip-hop, house, disco, R&B
Step 1

Feel the Syncopation.

Instead of hitting every beat, syncopated kicks land between beats. This creates tension against the steady snare.

  • Step 1 = Beat 1 (on the beat)
  • Step 3 = "e" of beat 1 (early, creates push)
  • Step 6 = "&" of beat 2 (after the snare)
  • Step 11 = "a" of beat 3 (anticipates beat 4)
Step 2

Build the Pattern.

Keep the snare on 2 & 4 and hi-hats on eighth notes as your anchor. The syncopated kick moves against these.

  • Kick: Steps 1, 3, 6, 11 (syncopated)
  • Snare: Steps 5 and 13 (steady backbeat)
  • Hi-Hat: Steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15 (eighth notes)

Listen: The kick "pulls" against the snare, creating tension that releases when the snare hits!

Exercise

Create a Syncopated Groove

Program a syncopated kick pattern with steady snare and hi-hats. Feel the groovy, off-beat movement!

What to do:

  1. Kick row: Click steps 1, 3, 6, 11 to place your syncopated kicks.
  2. Snare row: Click steps 5 and 13 to place your snares.
  3. Hi-Hat row: Click steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15 for eighth notes.
  4. Press Play to feel the groove.
  5. When satisfied, click Check Exercise to verify your pattern.
Tip: Syncopation = surprise! The steady snare/hi-hat anchors the groove while the kick adds movement.
