Lesson 1 · Drum pattern

4 on the floor: your first full beat.

In this lesson you build the classic 4 on the floor rhythm and, while you program it in your DAW or drum machine, you learn what bars, beats and subdivisions (1/4, 1/8, 1/16) are.

Free lesson
Drum basics
1-bar loop · 120 BPM
Structure Kick
Time 10–15 min
Goal
Understand counting
Program the kick pattern
Works in any DAW Ableton · FL · Logic · others
Step 1

Set up the project and learn the count.

One bar in 4/4 is divided into 4 beats. We count them like this: 1 – 2 – 3 – 4. Each number is a strong beat.

  • Set the tempo to 120 BPM.
  • Make sure the time signature is 4/4.
  • Turn on your DAW’s metronome.

  • Count out loud: 1 – 2 – 3 – 4 together with the metronome click.

When we talk about subdivisions: quarter notes (1/4) are the beats (1, 2, 3, 4), eighth notes (1/8) add the “&” (1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &), sixteenth notes (1/16) split each beat even further.

Step 2

Program the 4 on the floor.

Now create the classic 4 on the floor: a kick on every beat of the bar. This is the foundation of a huge amount of dance and pop music.

  • Create a 1-bar MIDI clip on your kick track.
  • Place a kick on beats 1, 2, 3, 4 (quarter-note grid).
  • Loop the clip and listen: you should hear a steady kick together with the metronome.
Step 3 · Exercise

Place the kicks in a 16-step bar.

Program the kick pattern in a 16-step bar divided into four beats. Each beat has 4 subdivisions (1/16 notes). Put kicks on steps 1, 5, 9, 13 for the classic 4-on-the-floor.
