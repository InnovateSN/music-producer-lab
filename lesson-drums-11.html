Concept

The Fastest Beats on Earth

Drum and bass (DnB) operates at 160-180 BPM—nearly twice as fast as most dance music. This high tempo creates incredible energy, but it also requires a different approach to programming. The patterns feel like they're running at half-speed because of how the snares are placed.

  • House tempo: 125 BPM - steady and grooving
  • Trap tempo: 140 BPM - hard-hitting and aggressive
  • DnB tempo: 174 BPM - fast but feels half-time
  • The half-time secret: At 174 BPM, snare on beats 2 and 4 feels like 87 BPM
History

The Legendary Breaks

DnB was built on sampled breakbeats from classic funk and soul records. The Amen Break (1969, The Winstons) is the most sampled drum break in history—its syncopated kick and crispy snare defined jungle and drum & bass.

  • Amen Break: The foundation of DnB, syncopated and crispy
  • Think Break: Funky and driving, great for liquid DnB
  • Apache Break: Open and spacious, works for half-time bass music
  • Two-step pattern: The core DnB rhythm with snare on 2 and 4
Technique

Programming the Two-Step

The two-step is DnB's core rhythm. The snare hits on beat 2 and 4 (at 174 BPM), creating that rolling, driving feel. The kick pattern is syncopated to add energy and movement.

  • Syncopated kicks: Off-beat placement creates rolling energy
  • Half-time snare: On beats 2 and 4 for that smooth feel
  • 8th note hi-hats: Drive the pattern forward at high speed
  • Variations: Add ghost notes and fills for human feel
Exercise

Build a Two-Step DnB Beat

Create a classic drum and bass two-step pattern with syncopated kick, backbeat snare, and driving 8th-note hi-hats at 174 BPM.

What to do:

  1. Kick: Steps 1, 7, 10 (syncopated for that rolling DnB feel)
  2. Snare: Steps 5 and 13 (the half-time backbeat on 2 and 4)
  3. Hi-Hat: Steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15 (8th notes for driving energy)
  4. Listen: At 174 BPM, it should feel rolling and energetic!
Tip: The kick pattern (1, 7, 10) creates the syncopated "rolling" feel that defines DnB. The snare on 2 and 4 makes it feel like half-time.
