Step 1

Oscillators: The sound source.

The oscillator generates the raw waveform‚Äîthe basic building block of your sound. Different waveforms have different harmonic content, which affects their character.

Sine
Pure, smooth, no harmonics
Saw
Bright, buzzy, all harmonics
Square
Hollow, odd harmonics
Triangle
Soft, odd harmonics

Tip: Saw waves are great for pads and leads. Square waves work well for bass. Sine waves are perfect for sub-bass.

Step 2

Filters: Shape the tone.

Filters remove frequencies from your sound. The most common is the low-pass filter (LPF), which removes high frequencies‚Äîessential for creating movement and warmth.

Key Filter Parameters
  • Cutoff ‚Äî The frequency where the filter starts working
  • Resonance ‚Äî Boosts frequencies around the cutoff (creates that classic synth "squelch")
  • Filter type ‚Äî LPF (bright‚Üídark), HPF (dark‚Üíbright), BPF (removes both ends)

Exercise: Load a saw wave and slowly sweep the LPF cutoff from high to low. Listen to how the sound changes from bright to dark.

Step 3

ADSR Envelopes: Shape over time.

Envelopes control how a parameter changes over time. The most common is the amplitude envelope, which shapes how your sound starts, holds, and ends.

  • Attack ‚Äî How fast the sound reaches full volume (0ms = instant, 500ms = slow fade in)
  • Decay ‚Äî Time to drop from peak to sustain level
  • Sustain ‚Äî The level held while key is pressed (not time!)
  • Release ‚Äî How long the sound fades after key release

Your task

Build a basic patch.

Open any synth (Vital, Serum, or your DAW's built-in synth) and create these three sounds:

  • A plucky lead: Saw wave, fast attack/decay, filter modulated by envelope
  • A warm pad: Two detuned saw waves, slow attack/release, low-pass filter
  • A solid bass: Square or saw wave, short envelope, low-pass filter to taste

Bonus: Try modulating the filter cutoff with a second envelope for more movement.

Key takeaways

What you've learned.

  • Oscillators generate raw waveforms with different harmonic content
  • Filters shape the tone by removing frequencies
  • ADSR envelopes control how sounds change over time
  • Combining these three elements creates infinite possibilities
