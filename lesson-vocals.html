Overview

The standard vocal chain.

Most professional vocal chains follow a similar order. Each step prepares the vocal for the next, building toward a polished, present sound.

Key Concept

Order matters!

The order of your vocal chain affects the final result. Always clean up first, then control dynamics, then enhance.

  • Cleanup before compression: Compressors amplify problems‚Äîremove them first
  • De-ess after compression: Compression can make sibilance worse
  • Effects on sends: Keep dry vocal intact, blend in wet signal
  • Less is more: Subtle moves add up to big results

Deep Dive

Compression settings explained.

Compression is the most important tool for controlling vocal dynamics. Here's what each parameter does:

  • Threshold: The level at which compression starts. Lower = more compression.
  • Ratio: How much to reduce signal above threshold. 4:1 is a good starting point.
  • Attack: How fast compression kicks in. Fast = catch peaks, slow = more natural.
  • Release: How fast compression lets go. Auto release often works well.
  • Makeup Gain: Bring the compressed signal back up to compensate for reduction.
