What Are Eighth Notes?

Subdividing the beat in half.

Eighth notes (1/8) divide each beat in half. If quarter notes are "1, 2, 3, 4", eighth notes add the "&" between: 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &.

  • Quarter notes: 4 per bar (the main beats).
  • Eighth notes: 8 per bar (twice as fast).
  • The "&": Falls exactly between the main beats.

Count along: "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &" ‚Äì the hi-hat plays on every syllable!

Step 1

Hi-Hats Add Motion and Energy.

Hi-hats are metallic cymbals that create rhythmic motion. They fill the space between kick and snare, giving the beat its energy and drive.

  • Short, crisp metallic sound that cuts through the mix.
  • Defines the tempo feel (straight or swung).
  • Can be "closed" (tight) or "open" (sustaining).
Step 2

Program the Eighth Notes Pattern.

In a 16-step grid, eighth notes fall on the odd-numbered steps: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15.

  • Keep your Kick: steps 1, 5, 9, 13.
  • Keep your Snare: steps 5 and 13.
  • Add Hi-Hat on: steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15.

  • Listen: The hi-hats create a steady "tss-tss-tss-tss" that drives the groove forward.

The result is a complete rock/pop beat with three elements working together!

Exercise

Add the Hi-Hats

Create a complete beat with kick, snare, and eighth-note hi-hats. This is your first full drum groove!

What to do:

  1. Kick row: Click steps 1, 5, 9, 13 to place your kicks.
  2. Snare row: Click steps 5 and 13 to place your snares.
  3. Hi-Hat row: Click steps 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15 (all odd steps).
  4. Press Play to hear your full groove.
  5. When satisfied, click Check Exercise to verify your pattern.
