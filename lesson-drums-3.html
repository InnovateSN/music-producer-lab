Step 1

Introducing the Hi-Hat.

Hi-hats are cymbals that mark faster subdivisions of the beat. They are essential to define the genre and energy of a rhythm.

  • The sound is short and metallic.
  • It adds rhythmic motion between kick and snare hits.
  • They typically play at 1/8 or 1/16 note speed.

In this lesson we’ll use a basic straight 1/8 note pattern to clearly hear the subdivisions between the main beats.

Step 2

Programming eighth notes (1 & 2 & ...).

If the kick hits on the quarters (1, 2, 3, 4), eighth notes land on both the number and the “and”: 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &.

  • Keep Kick (1, 5, 9, 13) and Snare (5, 13) as in Lesson 2.
  • Add a Hi-Hat on every eighth note.
  • In the 16-step grid, that means placing the Hi-Hat on all odd steps (1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15).

Listen to how straight eighth hats fill the rhythmic space and make the groove more energetic.

Step 3 · Exercise

Program the 1/8 Hi-Hat.

Your task is to complete the three-part groove. Kick and Snare are locked to the correct pattern from Lesson 2. You only need to program the Hi-Hat correctly.

Hi-Hat target: all odd steps (1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13, 15).
