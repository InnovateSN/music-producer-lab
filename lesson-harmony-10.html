House Music Harmony: Repetition with Sophistication

House music harmony is built on looping progressions that repeat hypnotically while maintaining sophistication through seventh chords and voice leading. You'll learn the viâ†’IVâ†’Iâ†’iii pattern in A minor - one of the most used progressions in deep house, tech house, and melodic techno.

The House Music Harmonic Formula

House music chord progressions have specific characteristics:

  • Seventh chords: Almost always use maj7, min7 for smoothness
  • 4 or 8-bar loops: Hypnotic repetition is the foundation
  • Sustained chords: Each chord typically lasts 2-4 beats
  • Voice leading: Smooth motion between chords, no big jumps
  • Simple melody: Topline targets chord tones, repeats with variation

Why This Progression Works in House

The viâ†’IVâ†’Iâ†’iii pattern is perfect for house music because:

Am7 (vi): Starts in minor - moody, introspective
Fmaj7 (IV): Lifts to major - brightness, release
Cmaj7 (I): Home chord - stability, resolution
Em7 (iii): Back to minor - sets up loop restart

This creates perpetual motion: start minor, brighten to major, resolve to home, return to minor darkness, repeat forever. The Em7 at the end pulls smoothly back to Am7 at the loop point. You hear this in Disclosure, Duke Dumont, Bicep.

Building the Chords

Let's construct each chord with seventh:

Am7 = A-C-E-G (i chord in A minor)
Fmaj7 = F-A-C-E (VI chord - brightness)
Cmaj7 = C-E-G-B (III chord - resolution)
Em7 = E-G-B-D (v chord - setup for loop)

Notice the smooth voice leading - many common tones:

  • A-C-E appears in both Am7 and Fmaj7
  • C-E-G appears in both Fmaj7 and Cmaj7
  • E-G-B appears in both Cmaj7 and Em7

These shared notes create smooth transitions. In your DAW, stack each chord vertically and sustain for 4 beats. Listen for how smoothly one chord flows into the next.

Adding the Topline Melody

House melodies are simple, repetitive, and lock with the chords:

Rule 1: Target chord tones on strong beats (beats 1 and 3)
Rule 2: Keep it minimal - 8-12 notes total for 4 bars
Rule 3: Repeat patterns with small variations
Rule 4: Stay in upper register for clarity over bassline

Production Tips for House Chords

When programming house chord loops:

  • Voicing: Play chords in C4-C5 range for warmth without muddiness
  • Sound selection: Rhodes, electric piano, warm synth pads work best
  • Rhythm: Chords can hit on beat 1 and sustain, or use gentle stabs
  • Velocity: Keep dynamics consistent - house is about groove, not expression
  • Effects: Add reverb and subtle chorus for space and shimmer

Arrangement approach:

  • Intro: Chords alone (8-16 bars)
  • Verse: Add bassline (I-VI-III-v root movement)
  • Build: Add drums and percussion
  • Drop: Full arrangement with melody on top

The chord loop is the foundation - everything else layers on top. Keep it tight, keep it grooving, let it repeat. Repetition isn't boring in house music - it's hypnotic.

Genre Variations

This progression appears across house subgenres with different treatments:

Deep House: Warm Rhodes, slow filter sweeps, sustained chords
Tech House: Minimal stabs, percussive chords, dry sound
Melodic Techno: Lush pads, arpeggiated, lots of reverb
Future House: Bright synths, bouncy rhythm, octave jumps

Same chords, different vibe. The harmonic foundation (viâ†’IVâ†’Iâ†’iii) stays constant, but production choices define the subgenre. Learn the chords first, then apply genre-specific sound design.
