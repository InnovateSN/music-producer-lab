The Off-Beat Hi-Hat Secret

The off-beat hi-hat is what gives house music its drive. Instead of playing on the beat (1, 2, 3, 4), it plays on the "and" counts (&):

ğŸ’¡ Pattern Variations

Once you've mastered the basic pattern, try these variations:

  • Add 16th hi-hats: Fill in more steps for a busier, driving feel
  • Remove kick on 3: Creates a "break" effect (common in tech house)
  • Add percussion: Shakers, congas, or rimshots on off-beats
  • Open hi-hat accents: Open hi-hat on beat 4 for tension before the loop restarts
  • Ride cymbal: Quarter-note ride for that "classic" sound

Key Takeaways

  • Four-on-the-floor kick is the foundation (steps 1, 5, 9, 13)
  • Off-beat hi-hats create forward momentum (steps 3, 7, 11, 15)
  • Clap/snare on 2 and 4 provides the backbeat
  • House: 120-128 BPM, Techno: 125-140 BPM, Deep House: 118-124 BPM
  • Less is more in minimal technoâ€”focus on groove, not complexity
