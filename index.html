Core Modules

Six production pillars, from rhythm to final master

Each module covers specific, testable skills: kick placement, sidechain compression, stereo width, loudness targets. Build your foundation, then layer in advanced techniques.

Drums & Rhythm

Program 4-on-the-floor patterns, offbeat hi-hats, and ghost snares. Learn swing timing, velocity layering, and rhythmic fills that drive energy.

Build Your First Beat (8 min)

Arrangement

Structure 8-bar intros, 16-bar buildups, and dynamic drops. Practice filtering, automation curves, and tension/release arcs for electronic and pop formats.

Structure a Full Track (12 min)

Sound Design

Shape sounds with oscillators, filters (low-pass, high-pass), ADSR envelopes, and LFOs. Layer samples, add distortion, and design bass, leads, and pads from scratch.

Design Your First Synth (10 min)

Mixing

Balance levels, carve space with EQ (cutting mud at 200-400Hz), apply compression ratios (4:1 for drums), and place elements in stereo field with panning and reverb sends.

Mix a Clean Balance (15 min)

Vocals

De-ess harsh frequencies, compress dynamics (2-4dB gain reduction), add doubles and harmonies, apply delay/reverb for depth. Make vocals clear and present without overpowering the mix.

Process a Vocal Track (12 min)

Mastering

Hit -14 LUFS for Spotify, apply multiband compression, stereo widening (keep bass mono), and peak limiting (0.3dB headroom). Prepare tracks for streaming and distribution.

Master for Streaming (10 min)

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you start.

How much does Music Producer Lab cost?

The basic labs are completely free. No credit card required, no registration needed. Open your browser and start. Some advanced labs require a subscription, but you already have 20+ free labs to get started.

Do I need a specific DAW?

No. Labs work in the browser - no installation needed. When you apply the skills to your DAW, the concepts work with Ableton, FL Studio, Logic, Bitwig, Reaper, any software. We teach universal principles, not program-specific tricks.

How long does it take to complete a lab?

Each lab takes 10-15 minutes on average. Some shorter (5 min), others longer (20 min). They're designed to be completed in a coffee break. You can do one lab a day or 5 in an afternoon - you set the pace.

Do I need to know music theory?

No. You don't need to read sheet music or know scales. We teach you what you need when you need it. If you need to program a bassline, we explain the right notes at that moment - learning by doing. Theory comes later, if you want to dive deeper.

Can I use the tracks I create in the labs?

Yes! Everything you produce in the labs is yours. You can publish it, remix it, use it as a foundation for bigger projects. The sounds provided in the labs are royalty-free for personal and commercial use.

Are there any age limitations?

There are no age limitations. Music production is for everyone - whether you're starting at 12 or 70. All you need is curiosity and willingness to experiment.

How is this different from YouTube tutorials?

YouTube teaches you concepts. Labs make you do the work. Instead of watching someone EQ a kick drum, you adjust the frequency knob yourself, hear the difference, make mistakes, and correct them. Active practice beats passive watching â€” that's proven learning science, not marketing.

What skills actually transfer to my DAW?

Everything fundamental: rhythm programming, gain staging, EQ frequency zones, compression ratios, reverb send levels, arrangement structure. DAWs have different interfaces but identical concepts. Once you understand "cut 200Hz to remove mud," you can do that in any software. Think of labs as flight simulators â€” different controls, same principles.

Why do interactive labs work better than courses?

Because you can't fake doing. In a video course, you can watch 10 hours and feel productive without building anything. Labs force output: every session ends with something you made. That immediate feedback loop (try â†’ hear â†’ adjust â†’ improve) builds skill faster than any amount of note-taking. Plus, you can't fall behind â€” work at your own pace.
