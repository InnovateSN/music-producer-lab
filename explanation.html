Free value · Lesson series

Drum patterns 101: start with solid grooves.

Begin with the essential 4 on the floor kick pattern. Then add snares, hi-hats, and ghost notes one layer at a time so you hear how a simple pulse becomes a full, modern groove without the overwhelm.

  • Understand what a bar is and how songs are counted (1–2–3–4).
  • Learn the difference between bars, beats and subdivisions (1/4, 1/8, 1/16).
  • See how a simple “kick on every beat” becomes full grooves with snares and hats.
  • Apply everything in any DAW: Ableton, FL Studio, Logic, or even simple drum machines.
Step-by-step exercises

Your first rhythm path.

Work through these short exercises in your own DAW or drum machine. No login, no account – just follow the steps and program the patterns.

  • Exercise 1 · Pure 4 on the floor – 1 bar, tempo 120 BPM, kick on every beat (1, 2, 3, 4).
  • Exercise 2 · Add the snare – keep the kicks, add snare on beats 2 and 4.
  • Exercise 3 · Straight 8th hi-hats – hats on every 1/8 note to feel subdivisions (1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &).
  • Exercise 4 · 16th-note groove – add extra hats on selected 1/16 notes for movement.
  • Exercise 5 · Variation & fills – change the last bar to create a simple fill before the loop repeats.

You can expand this series later into video, interactive labs or PDFs. The structure is already here so anyone can start learning drums for free.
